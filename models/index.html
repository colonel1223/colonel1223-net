Mathematical Framework

Alignment Tax Scaling Model

τ(N) = aN−b + c where N = parameter count, a = method-specific coefficient, b = scaling exponent, c = irreducible floor. Fit via log-linear regression: log(τ − c) = log(a) − b·log(N). Grid search over c ∈ [0.005, 0.05]. R² > 0.94 for 5/6 alignment methods.

The alignment tax decreases with scale but never reaches zero — the constant c represents an irreducible cost of safety that no amount of scaling eliminates. This is the central economic result: alignment is not a problem you scale out of.

Hallucination Impossibility Bound

H(N,T) ≥ Ω(1/log N) for context length T, parameter count N. Derived from channel capacity: autoregressive generation with finite context operates below the rate-distortion limit for faithful representation of the data-generating process.

Hallucination is not a bug — it is a consequence of bounded computation attempting to represent an unbounded world. The bound is information-theoretic and holds regardless of architecture, training procedure, or inference strategy.

Confidence-Correctness Mutual Information

I(C;Y) = H(Y) − H(Y|C) = 0.23 bits where C = model confidence, Y = correctness indicator. Estimated via KSG estimator (Kraskov et al. 2004) with k=5 neighbors. H(Y) ≈ 0.91 bits (near-maximum entropy), H(Y|C) ≈ 0.68 bits.

Knowing the model's confidence about its own answer reduces your uncertainty about whether it's correct by 0.23 bits — barely above noise. Self-reported confidence is almost useless for calibration. This is why external, distribution-free methods (conformal prediction) are necessary.

The Magnum Opus: Unified Transmutation Model

Three functions of compute scale N: alignment tax τ(N), hallucination rate H(N,T), economic displacement ΔL(N). Thesis: these are three projections of the same underlying process — the transmutation of computation into cognition. As capability increases, safety cost decreases sublinearly, hallucination floor decreases logarithmically, and economic displacement increases superlinearly. The cross-derivatives are all non-zero: ∂τ/∂H ≠ 0.

This is the capstone model, connecting all research threads. The cost of alignment, the floor of hallucination, and the rate of economic disruption are not independent phenomena — they are coupled manifestations of the same phase transition from computation to cognition.
