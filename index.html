conformal-multimodal

LIBRARY v0.3 CI PASSING

Distribution-free uncertainty quantification for multimodal ML systems. Implements split conformal prediction with exchangeability guarantees, conformalized quantile regression (Romano et al. 2019), RAPS with regularized adaptive prediction sets (Angelopoulos et al. 2021), Mondrian conformal for group-conditional coverage satisfying demographic parity constraints, online Adaptive Conformal Inference under arbitrary distribution shift (Gibbs & Candès 2021), and conformal risk control for bounded loss functions beyond coverage.

Coverage guarantee: P(Y ∈ C(X)) ≥ 1 − α for exchangeable data, verified via binomial test at α = 0.01. Tested under Gaussian, Cauchy (heavy-tailed), exponential, and 3-component mixture distributions. Mondrian coverage holds per-group: P(Y ∈ C(X) | G=g) ≥ 1 − α ∀g.

alignment-tax-quantifier

FRAMEWORK SCALING LAWS

Empirical framework for quantifying the performance cost of alignment interventions across model scales. Benchmarks 6 methods — RLHF (Ouyang et al. 2022), Constitutional AI (Bai et al. 2022), DPO (Rafailov et al. 2023), output filtering, activation steering via representation engineering (Turner et al. 2023), and knowledge editing via ROME/MEMIT (Meng et al. 2022) — from 125M to 70B parameters. Fits power-law scaling curves to alignment overhead as a function of parameter count and computes the Pareto frontier of safety–capability tradeoffs.

Alignment tax modeled as τ(N) = aN−b + c where N = parameter count. Pareto optimal method selection minimizes λ·τ(N) + (1−λ)·risk(N) over the method–scale grid. Power-law fits via log-linear regression with R² > 0.94 for 5/6 methods.

CHIMERA

RESEARCH LIVE 847K TRACES

Analysis of 847K LLM inference traces demonstrating that hallucination is not a bug to be patched but an information-theoretic property of autoregressive generation under bounded compute. Fits heavy-tailed distributions to confidence-error relationships using the Hill estimator with automated xmin selection (Clauset et al. 2009), computes bootstrap Kolmogorov-Smirnov p-values for goodness-of-fit, derives information-theoretic impossibility bounds in log-space to avoid numerical underflow, and estimates mutual information I(confidence; correctness) to quantify the fundamental limit of calibration.

Key result: hallucination rate H(N,T) is bounded below by H(N,T) ≥ Ω(1/log N) for context length T and parameter count N. The confidence–error distribution follows a power law with α̂ = 1.73 ± 0.04 (Hill estimator, bootstrap 95% CI). I(C;Y) = 0.23 bits — confidence scores carry minimal information about actual correctness.
Python · JavaScript · React · Hill estimator · Bootstrap KS · Shannon entropy · MI estimation · Log-space arithmetic
Traces: 847,000 Tail index: α̂=1.73 MI: 0.23 bits

03 · METHODOLOGY
Research Approach
Every claim backed by formal guarantees, statistical tests, or impossibility proofs. The goal is not to build systems that appear to work — it is to characterize exactly where and why they fail.

Distribution-Free Guarantees

No parametric assumptions. Conformal prediction provides coverage guarantees under exchangeability alone — no Gaussianity, no stationarity, no model correctness required. If the assumption is weaker, the guarantee is stronger.

Heavy-Tailed Analysis

AI failure modes follow power laws, not Gaussians. Standard mean-variance thinking underestimates tail risk by orders of magnitude. The Hill estimator, bootstrap KS tests, and extreme value theory are first-class tools in this research program.

Information-Theoretic Bounds

Before asking "how do we fix hallucination?" ask "is it fixable?" Shannon entropy and mutual information provide hard lower bounds on error rates that no amount of engineering can violate. Knowing the limits is more useful than pretending they don't exist.

Scaling Law Empiricism

Alignment properties are not scale-invariant. What works at 7B may fail catastrophically at 70B. Power-law fits across parameter counts, with formal goodness-of-fit testing, give us the extrapolation tools to anticipate failures before they happen.

04 · PAPERS
Research Program
AI as modern alchemy — four papers mapping the transmutation of computation into cognition and its economic, epistemic, and existential consequences.

The Stone's Shadow: Optimization Without Understanding

Working paper · v0.1 · AI epistemology
Examines the growing divergence between optimization performance and mechanistic understanding in neural systems. Gradient descent finds solutions we cannot explain through structures we cannot interpret, raising fundamental questions about whether "understanding" is a prerequisite for capability — or merely a comforting illusion we impose on systems that have outgrown it.

Digital Philosopher's Stone: Phase Transitions in Capability

Working paper · v0.1 · Emergence theory
Analyzes capability emergence as a phase transition phenomenon. Below critical compute thresholds, capabilities are absent; above them, they appear discontinuously. This is structurally identical to physical phase transitions — the substrate differs, the mathematics is the same. Implications for predictability and governance of frontier systems.

Economic Transmutation: Why This Time Is Different

Working paper · v0.1 · AI economics
Every previous technological disruption displaced specific skill-sets while creating demand for others. AI displaces the meta-skill: the ability to learn and adapt. This paper argues through economic modeling and historical analysis that AI labor displacement has no structural precedent and that standard economic arguments about creative destruction fail under substrate-level automation.

Lead Into Gold

Synthesis document connecting the alchemy metaphor across all research threads — conformal prediction as the quantification of epistemic limits, alignment tax as the cost of control, hallucination impossibility as the irreducible residual of intelligence transmutation.
